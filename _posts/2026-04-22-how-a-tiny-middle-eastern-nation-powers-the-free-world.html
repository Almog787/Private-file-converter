The Food on Your Table and the Water in Your Tap

The Western world is currently facing unprecedented climate challenges. Severe droughts in places like California and Southern Europe are threatening agricultural yields. When crop yields drop, the price of your groceries skyrockets at the supermarket. This is basic supply and demand.

Israel, geographically composed mostly of harsh desert, faced this existential threat decades ago. Out of necessity, they invented modern drip irrigation—a technology that allows crops to grow in arid environments using a fraction of the water. Today, this technology is exported globally, stabilizing food supplies in the US and Europe. Furthermore, Israel leads the world in water desalination and recycling, reclaiming nearly 90% of its wastewater for agriculture. By sharing this agricultural and climate technology with the West, Israel actively helps keep the global supply of fresh produce stable, which directly prevents your grocery bill from climbing even higher.
